Positron Shooter

The Positron Shooter shoots orange lasers. In its initial stage, it has a very low range, and its projectiles are affected by gravity. It can be charged by holding up and pressing fire repeatedly, at which point it will play a melody. Partially charging the gun will result in its range increasing relative to the amount of charge, and will decrease how much the projectiles are affected by gravity. Shooting it in its partially charged state will reduce its total charge slightly with each shot. A fully charged Positron Shooter will shoot a wide beam projectile that destroys walls and starts fires, but will use up the gun's entire charge in the process. If overcharged, the gun will automatically release a fully charged shot, but will force the user into a ragdoll state and launch them backwards.

The gun is a reference to a Futurama episode.
